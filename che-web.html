Land-related Issues

Deforestation:

Deforestation refers to the large-scale removal or clearing of forests or trees.
Often for commercial purposes or to make way for other land uses such as agriculture,urban development, or infrastructure projects.
It is a significant environmental issue with widespread and far-reaching consequences.
Learn More


Soil Degradation:

Soil degradation refers to the decline in the quality, fertility, and overall health of the soil.
It involves the deterioration of soil functions and its ability to support plant and animal life.
Soil degradation is a complex process influenced by natural factors, such as climate and geological processes, as well as human activities.
It can manifest in various forms, impacting the physical, chemical, and biological properties of the soil..
Learn More


Land Pollution:

Land pollution refers to the degradation of the Earth's surface and soil due to the introduction of harmful substances, contaminants, and pollutants.
This form of pollution negatively affects the quality of the land, making it less suitable for agriculture, ecosystems, and human habitation.
Land pollution can result from various human activities and industrial processes, leading to widespread environmental and health issues.
Learn More


Urbanization and Infrastructure Development:

Urbanization and infrastructure development bring about both positive and negative impacts.
While they contribute to economic growth, improved living standards, and better access to amenities,
they also pose significant challenges and negative consequences like Environmental Degradation,
Resource Depletion,Infrastructure Challenges,Social and Economic Disparities.Climate Change Impact,Natural Disaster Vulnerability.
Learn More


Water Scarcity:

Water scarcity refers to a situation where the demand for freshwater exceeds the available amount during a certain period or when poor quality limits its use.
It's a critical global issue resulting from various factors and leading to significant challenges for human populations and ecosystems.
Learn More


Desertification:

Desertification is the process by which land in drylands (arid, semi-arid and dry sub-humid areas) becomes progressively degraded, usually due to climate variability and human activities.
It is a type of land degradation that results in the loss of biological productivity and the depletion of natural resources.
