Causes of Desertification

Climate change

Climate variability and anthropogenic climate change, particularly through increases in both land surface air temperature and evapotranspiration, and decreases in precipitation, are likely to have played a role, in interaction with human activities, in causing desertification in some dryland areas.

Overgrazing

Overgrazing is the practice of grazing livestock on land at a rate that exceeds the capacity of the land to support them.

This can lead to the removal of vegetation, which exposes the soil to erosion. Overgrazing can also damage the soil structure, which can reduce its ability to retain water.

Deforestation

Deforestation is the clearing of forests for other uses, such as agriculture, development, or logging.

Forests play an important role in regulating the water cycle and preventing erosion. When forests are cleared, the land becomes more susceptible to desertification.
