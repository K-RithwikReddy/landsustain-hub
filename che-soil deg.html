Types of Floods

Soil Erosion

Soil erosion is the process by which the topsoil is removed by natural agents like water and wind, leading to the loss of fertile soil.

This causes deforestation, improper agricultural practices, overgrazing, and construction activities contribute to soil erosion. It Reduces soil fertility, diminished water quality, and increased susceptibility to flooding.

Soil Compaction

Soil compaction is the compression of soil particles, reducing pore space and hindering the movement of air, water, and nutrients.

The causes are Heavy machinery, intensive agricultural practices, and construction activities can lead to soil compaction.

Salinization

Salinization occurs when salts accumulate in the soil, adversely affecting plant growth and soil fertility.Causes Over-irrigation, poor drainage, and the use of saline water for irrigation can contribute to salinization.

Has impacts such as Decreased crop yields, impaired plant health, and compromised soil structure
