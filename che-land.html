Types of Land Pollution

Chemical Pollution

Chemical pollution involves the introduction of harmful chemicals and toxic substances into the soil.

Sources: Industrial discharges, improper disposal of hazardous waste, and the use of pesticides and fertilizers in agriculture. Effects: Soil contamination, reduced fertility, and potential harm to plants, animals, and humans.

Plastic pollution

Plastic pollution occurs when plastic materials accumulate in the soil and persist for extended periods.

Sources: Improper disposal of plastic waste, littering, and inadequate recycling. Effects: Soil degradation, hindrance of plant growth, and harm to wildlife that may ingest or become entangled in plastic debris.

Soil contaminatio

Soil contamination involves the presence of hazardous substances in the soil, affecting its natural composition.

Sources: Industrial activities, spills, leaks, and improper disposal of chemicals and pollutants. Effects: Impaired soil fertility, toxicity to plants and microorganisms, and potential harm to ecosystems.
